HISTORY

History of Granada

Granada, with its roots traced back to ancient civilizations, has a rich and captivating history. The Nasrid dynasty, ruling from the Alhambra, contributed to the city's architectural marvels, leaving an indelible
mark on the landscape.

The Alhambra, a UNESCO World Heritage site, stands as a testament to Moorish architecture and the artistic achievements of the Nasrids. Explore the intricate details of the Generalife gardens and the stunning Nasrid Palaces.

The Renaissance Period

After the Reconquista, the Renaissance brought a new chapter to Granada. The Royal Chapel, the final resting place of Catholic Monarchs Ferdinand and Isabella, and the Granada Cathedral showcase the grandeur of this era.

Explore the elegant streets of the city center and marvel at the Renaissance architecture. The vibrant Plaza Nueva and the bustling Calle Elvira are perfect spots to soak in the Renaissance ambiance.

Modern Granada

Discover the modern face of Granada, where contemporary art, cuisine, and innovation thrive. The Science Park and the Granada Conference and Exhibition Center are symbols of the city's forward-looking spirit.

Stroll through the trendy Realejo district and experience the blend of old and new. Granada's modern landscape complements its rich history, creating a city that embraces the past and embraces the future.
